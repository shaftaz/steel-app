Chinese HRC export offers just rose to $488–494/t FOB. Here's why buyers shouldn't care yet.

The move tracked a two-session futures rally — not orders. Overseas inquiries: flat. Deals: thin. Southeast Asia: mid-lull.

We call this the validation gap: the distance between where sellers mark the market and where demand will actually transact. In April, the same pattern resolved with falling export orders, not rising prices.

Rule of thumb: futures move in sessions; demand moves in seasons. Watch the inquiries, not the offers.
