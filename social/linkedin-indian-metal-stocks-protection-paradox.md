# LinkedIn draft — indian-metal-stocks-protection-paradox (do not publish on site)

The Sensex jumped 965 points Friday. Metal stocks sat it out.

Strange? Not really. India's steelmakers sit behind a 12% safeguard duty that cut import volumes ~33% — and their stocks still trade with the global cycle, because tariffs protect volumes, not margins. With IREPAS calling the long steel market weaker again and fresh Iranian + Russian billet heading for the market, equities are pricing the world price, not the duty schedule.

We call it the protection paradox: the weaker global steel gets, the more your safeguard defends share — and the less it defends profit.
