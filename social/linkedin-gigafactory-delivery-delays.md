# LinkedIn draft — gigafactory-delivery-delays (do not publish on site)

The battery market just inverted — and most gigafactory owners haven't noticed.

For a decade, a late factory was forgiven. Demand queued up; everything eventually sold. Now global cell capacity is past 2,500 GWh against ~1.6 TWh of demand, prices fell ~50% in a year, and 220 GWh of the 2030 pipeline is already cancelled or postponed.

In that market, a 6-month SOP slip at a 40 GWh plant isn't deferred revenue. It's ~$250M at risk — and possibly volume your competitor ships instead.

The leaders are building in 18–24 months while the average project slips 6–9 months past its own plan. We call that gap the execution spread — and it's now worth more than most technology bets.
